What Do I Need to Get Started?

  • A sunny location that gets at least 6-8 hours of sunlight per day.

  • Depending on your space allotment: lots of pots (most veggies do well in pots!), raised beds (wood is ideal) or a spot on the ground that has been tilled and is free from grass and weeds

  • Good soil and tons of COMPOST
    • What is compost? Organic material (veggie scraps, yard clippings, etc) that has been naturally broken down into super nutrient-dense soil.
    • Where do I get it? Any local gardening supply shop will have some. Or you can make your own!

  • Mulch! This will go on top of your soil, around the plants, to hold in moisture that evaporates quickly in the Texas heat.

  • Seeds or transplants. If you are just getting into growing, transplants are the way to go. Your local garden shop will have anything available that’s in season.

  • A water source. Even, consistent watering is important for growing food so, make sure you set a watering schedule for yourself. You could also set up an automated timer system through your outdoor faucet or sprinkler system. Always check the weather to see when Mother Nature might give you some FREE water!

  • Plant food. Plants need to eat just like we do! Set yourself a feeding schedule so your plants don't go hungry.
